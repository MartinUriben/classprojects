What is Ajiaco?

Ajiaco is a traditional Colombian soup, especially popular in Bogotá. It's rich, hearty, and made with a variety of ingredients.

Ingredients Required

  • Chicken (bone-in, skinless pieces): About 4 chicken thighs or breasts.
  • Papa criolla (small yellow potatoes): 8-10 small ones, typically mashed into the soup.
  • Papa sabanera (red potatoes): 4-5, thinly sliced.
  • Papa pastusa (white potatoes): 4-5, thinly sliced.
  • Corn on the cob: 2-3 ears, cut into thirds.
  • Guascas (a key herb in Ajiaco): 2-3 tablespoons of dried guascas.
  • Capers: For garnish, to taste.
  • Heavy cream: ½ cup, for serving.
  • Avocados: 1-2, sliced, for serving.
  • Chicken stock or water: About 8 cups (for boiling and soup base).

Cooking Instructions

Cook the Chicken

  1. In a large pot, add the chicken thighs (or breasts) and pour in the chicken stock (or water) until they are fully submerged.
  2. Bring the stock to a boil over medium-high heat.
  3. Once boiling, reduce the heat to medium-low, cover, and simmer the chicken for about 30 minutes or until cooked through and tender.
  4. Remove the chicken from the pot and set it aside. Shred the chicken once it cools slightly, and reserve for later.

Add the Potatoes

  1. To the same pot with the chicken stock, add the papa criolla, papa sabanera, and papa pastusa.
  2. Add the corn pieces to the pot as well.
  3. Season the mixture with salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer and let it cook for about 30-40 minutes. The papa criolla will break down and thicken the soup, while the other potatoes will remain chunkier.

Add the Guascas

  1. When the potatoes have softened, add the guascas to the pot.
  2. Stir and let the soup simmer for another 10-15 minutes so the flavors can meld together.

Shred and Return the Chicken

  1. While the soup continues to cook, shred the chicken into bite-sized pieces
  2. Add the shredded chicken back into the pot and mix well.

Final Adjustments

  1. Taste the soup and adjust seasoning as needed (more salt, pepper, or guascas).
  2. Let the soup simmer for another 5-10 minutes to blend flavors.
  3. When serving Ajiaco be sure to use hot bowls.
  4. On the side you can provide bowls of capers, cream, and avocado slices so people can add them as desired.
  5. White rice also makes for a delicious topping.
Enjoy this Delicious Soup!
